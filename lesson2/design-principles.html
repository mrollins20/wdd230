White space and Clean Design

Crunchyroll

Crunchyroll Beta Website

I find the new webpage style that Crunchyroll is trying to achieve with their beta webpage is a great example of many design principles; Though I'm going to focus on how they utilize their white space and clean design.

To start, they do an excellent job of dividing all of their webpage into distinct sections. They have a navigation bar at the top that is easy to read and understand, a large central image that cycles through other news and images. In between these sections, they have a black background that is being used as their white space.They also use lines inbetween some of their elements to create breaks in their content and provide a bit of color variation.

Visual Hierarchy

Steam

Steam Storepage

Steam is one of those webpages that just makes sense. Everything is in a form of visual hierarchy for presentation. They have something that catches the eye on every new section to pull the user into more of the content. They utilize good breaking points and a page banner for announcing the top products. Another aspect of visual hierarchy is the small, yet effective, use of a slider indicator and the more button on all their scrolling content. Most importantly, I believe their color-scheme plays a huge role in the webpage's hierarchy; By utilizing more neutral colors the products and ads shown standout way more.

Repetition

Youtube

Youtube Homepage

Youtube is a standard when it comes to repetition. They utilize every detail of their work over and over again. When you look at their home page they have every single video thumbnail and title listed one after another. They have a simple navigation bar that repeats to the right and a similar bar at the bottom of their webpage. I believe their repetition also looks to good because they do a good job of balance all of their elements on each half of the screen.
